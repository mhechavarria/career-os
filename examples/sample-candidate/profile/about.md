# About

I'm a backend engineer with a little over a decade of experience building and operating
distributed systems — from the firmware on a warehouse robot to event-driven services
ingesting telemetry from tens of thousands of sensors. My career started in embedded
software, moved into backend product work, and has settled into cloud-native platform
engineering with a recent focus on AI-augmented developer tooling.

What ties it together is a systems instinct: I like understanding a problem end to end,
from the wire protocol up through the API to the dashboards that tell us it's healthy.
I care about the boring parts that keep services alive — deployment safety, observability,
backpressure, and the cost of a query at 3am — at least as much as the features on top.

These days I work mostly in Go and Python on Kubernetes and AWS, designing services around
Kafka and Postgres, and instrumenting everything with OpenTelemetry and Datadog. Most
recently I built an LLM-based incident-triage assistant that cut our mean time to a first
diagnosis by more than half.

I'm looking for senior backend or platform roles on remote-first teams with real
distributed-systems problems, a healthy on-call culture, and an engineering org that
treats reliability as a feature.
