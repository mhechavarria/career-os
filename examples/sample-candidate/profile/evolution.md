# Career Evolution

## Phase 1 — Embedded & Firmware (2013–2015)

Started at Vela Robotics writing C/C++ firmware for warehouse robots and the Python
tooling around them. Learned to debug across the hardware/software boundary and shipped
the first telemetry pipeline that put device data into the cloud.

## Phase 2 — Backend Product Engineering (2015–2019)

At Tessera Health and Coriolis Analytics I moved up the stack into Java and then
Node.js/TypeScript backends — REST APIs, relational data modelling, and my first
real-time, Kafka-based streaming systems. This is where I learned to design for
throughput and for failure.

## Phase 3 — Cloud-Native & Distributed Systems (2019–2022)

At Nimbus Freight I owned services on GCP and Kubernetes for a logistics platform —
event sourcing, asynchronous workers, and the operational discipline of running
multi-region systems that move real money and freight.

## Phase 4 — Platform & AI-Augmented Engineering (2022–present)

At Heliograph I work on the ingestion and platform layer of a climate-data product:
Go services on EKS, Kafka, Terraform, and a strong observability practice. The newest
thread is AI-augmented tooling — using Anthropic Claude via AWS Bedrock to take toil
out of incident response and code review.

## The thread

Every phase has been about the same thing from a different altitude: making systems
observable, reliable, and cheap to operate. The hardware taught me to respect physics
and failure; the cloud taught me to design for it at scale.
