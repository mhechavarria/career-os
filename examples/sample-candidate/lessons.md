# Lessons — the Judgment Flywheel

> Jordan's accumulated job-search lessons — the compounding half of the loop. See the
> repo-root `lessons.md` for how capture → distill → apply works. _(Example data —
> generalized principles, not tied to any real company.)_

## Lessons

- **Qualify location, comp, and role-type before building anything.** A hard-filter miss —
  a role that turns out to be residency-restricted, or below your comp floor — sinks the
  entire research-and-CV investment. Run the Phase 7.0 triage gate first, every time; the
  cheapest win is not starting a build you can't finish.
- **Name the gap before they find it.** Interviews go better when you've pre-written the
  honest answer to the role's weakest requirement than when you dodge it. "Here's the
  adjacent experience and how fast I'd ramp" reads as senior; a surprised non-answer reads
  as a bluff called.
- **Read the interviewer before choosing which story to lead with.** The same experience
  can be told as a reliability story or an architecture story. Leading with the one that
  matches the interviewer's background (an ex-SRE → the reliability story) landed
  noticeably better than telling them in a fixed order.
